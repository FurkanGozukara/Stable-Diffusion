Step back into August 1997 for one of the most pivotal and historic moments in the history of technology. In this monumental Macworld Boston keynote, a recently returned Steve Jobs takes the stage to deliver a brutally honest status report on a struggling Apple Computer—and outlines the radical steps required to save the company from the brink of bankruptcy.

Introduced by Mac Publications CEO Colin Crawford, Steve Jobs addresses the rumors of Apple's demise and lays out a masterful blueprint for the company's revival. He identifies Apple's core strengths, introduces a powerhouse new Board of Directors, and refocuses the company on its most loyal user bases: Creative Professionals and the Education sector.

However, the most shocking moment of the presentation comes when Jobs announces a sweeping, historic partnership with Apple's biggest rival: Microsoft. Featuring a live satellite appearance by Bill Gates, this announcement officially ended the "Apple vs. Microsoft" war of the 1990s and secured a vital $150 million investment that helped keep Apple alive.

Jobs closes the speech with the profound philosophy that "for Apple to win, Microsoft does not have to lose," planting the early seeds for the legendary "Think Different" era.
